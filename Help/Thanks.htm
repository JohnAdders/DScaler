Thanks 
 

See Authors for up to date list of developers who have contributed code to the project.

See Thanks_MultiDec.txt for a list of thanks for the project DScaler was based upon.

  1. A big debt a gratitude is due to Mark Rejhon for his 3:2 algorithm and his help testing, promoting DScaler but particularly for his vision and his refusal to accept second best.
  2. Thanks to Michael Samblanet, who contributed the aspect ratio control code.
  3. Thanks to Daniel Schmelzer for his hard work creating and updating the website. 
  4. Thanks to Steve Grimm for his work on the new and old methods of 3:2 pulldown and to his contribution of the aspect ratio autodetect code.
  5. Thanks to Tom Barry for his implementation of triple buffering and his synchronization fix, as well as his contribution of low-level user manipulation of the bt chipset.
  6. Thanks to Yvon Quere and Ali for finding, using and promoting bTV and Yvon for creating and updating the FAQ.
  7. Thanks to Jason Castle for updating the todo and bugs pages.
  8. Thanks to Borg, creator of bTV, for writing the first ever intelligent computer deinterlacing program--the inspiration for this project.
  9. Thanks to all at the AVSForums for your encouragement and help. The HTPC forum is easily the best group on the internet.
  10. Thanks to the authors of VirtualDub for the the idea behind the PAL detection algorithm.
  11. Thanks to Gunnar Thalin for his area based deinterlacing plug-in which was the basis for the Video Bob deinterlacing method.
  12. Thanks to Steaphan Greene, Øyvind Aabling, Octavian Purdila, Vladimir Dergachev and Christian Lupien—the GATOS developers—for the original screen capture code.
  13. Thanks to Torbjörn Jansson for his bug fixes.
  14. Thanks to Erik Språng for the new DScaler logo.
  15. Thanks to Phat Phreddy for the new DScaler domains.
  16. Thanks to Tom Fojta for the Czech teletext code page.
  17. Thanks to John Isar for the Greek teletext code page.
  18. Thanks to Eduardo José Tagle for the PAL-NC patch.
  19. Finally thanks to everybody on the deinterlace project mailing list.
